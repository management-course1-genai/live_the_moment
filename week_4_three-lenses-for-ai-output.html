The evaluator's eye:
three lenses for any AI output

AI writes so fluently that it's easy to be charmed by an answer that's quietly wrong, off-topic, or unusable. The fix isn't technical — it's a habit of looking through three simple lenses. You'll practise each one by grading real outputs, then meet the trick that scales it up.

1Lens one

Faithfulness — does it stay true to the input?

When AI summarises, extracts, or rewrites something, the first question is simple: did it keep the facts and honour the constraints from the source? Lens one, in full
Faithfulness

Does the output stay true to the input? Are facts preserved? Are constraints honoured? Faithfulness is the most important lens in any setting where the model is summarising, extracting, or transforming source material — because a fluent summary that flips a "no refund" into a "refund" is worse than no summary at all.

Lens two, in full
Relevance

Does the output actually address what was asked? Fluency and accuracy are not the same as relevance — an answer can be both polished and correct while solving the wrong problem entirely. The lens is simple to apply once you name it: re-read the original ask, then check the answer against it word for word.

The brief — the format rules
Write a team status update following these rules:
  • Exactly 3 bullet points.
  • Each bullet under 15 words.
  • Each bullet starts with a label: DONE, DOING, or BLOCKED.
  • End with a reference code in the form REF-#### (four digits).

Lens three, in full
Format compliance

Did the output follow the structure you asked for — length, tone, sections, fields, citations? All of these are part of the brief. An output that ignores the format is unusable downstream, even if the content is good: a perfect answer in the wrong shape still has to be redone by hand.

Definition
LLM-as-judge

A pragmatic evaluation pattern in which one AI model scores the output of another (or its own) against a rubric you design. It is not a substitute for human judgement on the hardest cases, but it is invaluable for scaling routine quality checks across thousands of outputs.

The whole picture

Three lenses, one habit: ask whether an output is faithful to its source, relevant to the ask, and compliant with the format. Run them yourself on the cases that matter, and hand the routine, high-volume checks to an AI judge — while keeping the final word, on anything genuinely ambiguous, firmly human.
